The Modularization Quality (MQ)

The MQ of an Module Dependency Graph (MDG) that is partitioned into k clusters (packages) is the difference between the average inter- and intra-connectivity of the k clusters (packages).

The intra-connectivity Ai of cluster i consisting of Ni nodes and Mi intra-edges is the fraction of Mi over the maximum number of intra-edges of i (i.e. Ni2).
The inter-connectivity Eij between two distinct clusters i and j consisting of Ni and Nj nodes, respectively, and with Eij inter-edges is the fraction of Eij over the maximum number of inter-edges between i and j (i.e. 2*Ni*Nj).

MQ is calculatable for the project node only, for the package nodes the intra-connectivity value is shown.

In RefactorIT Metrics original MQ is scaled thousand times in the results table, so the range is -1000..1000.

The MQ can not be used to compare different projects, but to show the development trend of the given project, e.g. to see if the Move Class improved the structure of the project. In general improving the structure of the project causes MQ to grow.
